# CubeSat Solar Panel Appendage Release System

**Organization:** Grupo de Tecnología Aeroespacial, UTN (2022–2024)
**Recognition:** Best Presentation Award in Satellite Technology — IAA Latin American Small Satellite Conference, 2024

This project involved the design, simulation, and experimental characterization of a solar panel deployment mechanism for a CubeSat. The work was developed within UTN's aerospace research group and culminated in an international conference presentation.

---

## What was built

Solar panels on a CubeSat are stowed during launch to comply with volume constraints, then deployed once in orbit. The mechanism that enables this — the appendage release system — must be reliable, lightweight, compact, and capable of surviving launch vibration loads without premature activation.

The project covered the full development cycle of such a mechanism: mechanical design, simulation of the deployment kinematics and structural behavior, and physical characterization of the release process.

---

## Key challenges

Designing a deployment mechanism for space involves constraints that don't appear in typical mechanical design:

- **Volume and mass**: CubeSats operate under strict dimensional and mass budgets. Every component must justify its presence.
- **Single-shot reliability**: The mechanism deploys once. There is no second chance. Failure tolerance must be designed in, not assumed.
- **Launch environment**: The structure must survive vibration loads during launch without releasing prematurely, then release cleanly on command in orbit.
- **Thermal and vacuum behavior**: Materials and mechanisms that work on a bench may behave differently in the temperature cycling and vacuum of low Earth orbit.

---

## Results and recognition

The work was presented at the **IAA Latin American Small Satellite Conference (2024)**, where it received the **Best Presentation Award in Satellite Technology**. The conference brought together researchers and engineers from across Latin America working on small satellite systems.
