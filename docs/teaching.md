# Teaching & Visualization Work

Teaching has been part of my life since 2019. Over 7 years of one-on-one tutoring in mathematics and physics, and now as a Teaching Assistant in Control Systems at UTN, I've consistently found that the act of explaining something forces a deeper understanding of it than just studying it alone. I build tools to help with that — for myself first, then for others.

---

## Teaching Assistant — Control Systems, UTN (2025–Present)

I assist in the Control Systems course for Aerospace Engineering students at UTN, with a focus on:

- AI-based control methods and their aerospace applications
- System dynamics and simulation
- Control theory fundamentals

The course directly connects to my research interests, which lets me bring real problems and examples into the material rather than working purely with textbook cases.

## Private Tutor — Mathematics & Physics (2019–Present)

Seven years of one-on-one tutoring across a wide range of levels — from high school students facing their first calculus problems to university students working through differential equations and classical mechanics.

What I've found is that sustained demand from students — through referrals and returning students — is the most honest feedback a tutor can get. Students choose whether to come back, and whether to send their friends. That consistency over seven years tells me more about the quality of the work than any single session could.

One case stays with me. A student with significant learning difficulties had been struggling with mathematics and physics for a long time. After working together, he achieved the best score in his class on an exam. More than the grade, he told me he wanted to pursue a future in mathematics or physics. That kind of outcome is what makes teaching worth doing.

## Visualization Tools: Manim Animations for Joukowski Profiles

I built a set of animations using the [Manim](https://www.manim.community/) library (the same engine behind 3Blue1Brown's videos) to visualize **Joukowski conformal mappings** — a classical technique in aerodynamics that uses complex-plane transformations to analyze airfoil pressure distributions.

The Joukowski transform maps a circle in the complex plane to an airfoil-shaped profile. Visualizing *why* this works — watching the circle deform continuously into a wing shape, tracking how points on the circle correspond to points on the profile — builds a geometric intuition that equations alone don't provide. I made these animations because I wanted that intuition for myself.

After developing them, I shared the animations with UTN to use as teaching material in aerodynamics courses.

## Why I think about teaching the way I do

The best explanation I can give of a concept is one that makes the listener think "of course — it couldn't work any other way." Getting there usually requires understanding the thing deeply enough to see why it's true, not just that it is. That's the standard I try to hold myself to, both in teaching and in my own work.
